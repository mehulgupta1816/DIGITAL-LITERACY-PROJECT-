Twitter Bitcoin Scandal (Phishing Attack) July 2020

The Phishing attack at Twitter in July 2020 was one of the highest profile phishing attacks to date and successfully hacked into the accounts of some of the biggest names in the world including an account belonging to Elon Musk, Barack Obama, and Bill Gates, among others. The Twitter Phishing Attack is a perfect example of how sophisticated social engineering techniques can evolve and target major platforms.

The Twitter phishing attack began with social engineers targeting Twitter employees through Phishing calls (a Technique known as “Vishing”).

In total, the Twitter phishing attack proceeded as follows:

1. The attackers contacted Twitter employees posing as IT staff.
2. The attackers convinced the employees to divulge their internal system login credentials.
3. The attackers then used these login credentials to access the Twitter Admin tools.
4. The attackers then took control of some of the verified user accounts belonging to celebrities and businesses.
5. The attackers posted fraudulent tweets to the hacked accounts asking for users to send in Bitcoins, doubling what they sent in return.

The attack targets were both employees (for account access) and members of the general public (for Theft). The attackers used the trust that many people have for verified accounts to make their fraud appear legitimate.

The consequences were significant. High-profile accounts were misused, causing global panic and reputational damage to Twitter. Thousands of users sent money, leading to financial losses. The incident also raised serious concerns about platform security and internal access controls.

It is an Illustrative example of how phishing is not just limited to email attacks, but also includes Social engineering Behavior via phone calls. The Twitter Phishing Attack also illustrates the need for strong employee training, Multi-Factor Authentication, and strict access controls to prevent such large-scale attacks.
