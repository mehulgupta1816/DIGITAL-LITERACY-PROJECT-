For this task, I used Canva to design my infographic because it is easy to use and offers many templates and design elements. My visual slide is titled “Digital Literacy for Students.” It covers three main areas: the meaning of digital literacy, useful digital tools, and safe internet practices. I included simple definitions, relevant icons, and examples like Google Docs, Canva, and Zoom to make the content practical and relatable for students. The design focuses on clarity and visual appeal. It uses minimal text and a consistent color scheme.

While creating the infographic, I discovered how important visual hierarchy is for making information easy to understand. Arranging elements properly improved readability. However, I found it a bit challenging to balance the amount of information with design simplicity; adding too much text made the layout look cluttered. Overall, this task helped me understand both digital literacy concepts and basic design principles.
